a.  Start in the prone position.

b.  From the prone position, slowly elevate the upper body leaving the pelvis on the floor. This should put a stretch on the low back.

Notes: This position is extremely gravity dependent on the low back and should be done very carefully.

Risk factors:

  1. The stretch is felt primarily in the low back, but also in the groin areas. If a stretch can be felt more in one groin than the other, this is considered a risk factor.
  2. If it can be felt on one side of the lower back more than the other, this also is a risk factor.
